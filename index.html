my first tech conference experience

#signalconf 2017

May.25.2017

It's been a couple weeks since the big gathering at Pier 27 and here is my take away on the SIGNAL convention. I was a part of Twilio’s first Scholarship Program! Thank goodness, because I don’t think I would have been able to comfortably afford the ticket price on a college student budget. Most people were sent there by their company, to see what’s in and new with tech. What’s twilio’s next greatest API? I even met a girl who’s in market research, her job was to chat with the developers attending this conference. and write on why the tech scene is so hot.

This was my very first tech convention, and not gonna lie - it was quite overwhelming. I felt like a little guppy in the big blue San Francisco sea. They weren’t kidding when they said there are not a lot of women in tech. Everyone I met seems to go… “Hello my name is ________, and I do, Buzzword Buzzword Buzzword. I wanted to wear a nametag that says “Hello, I’m new, please speak slowly and softly.”

The second day was a lot better, there was a Twomen lunch! Basically a Twilio's women's Employee lunch event. A.k.a. A more comfortable space, to not be awkward, and talk to fellow female developers. I did enjoy this day a lot more. I didn’t feel so out of place. I remember a wise female developer once told me: “You won't always connect with everyone you talk to, but don't let that stop you.”

And that is so true - Don't be afraid to talk to people. Through this Signal conference experience, after breaking out of my comfort zone, I have made connections that will last a lifetime, and I learned all kinds of things I can carry with me on my journey in technology.

You won't always connect with everyone you talk to, but don't let that stop you.
